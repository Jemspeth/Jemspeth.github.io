Jeremy Speth

Email | CV | LinkedIn | Google Scholar

I am currently a Senior Research Scientist at Presage Technologies working on camera-based blood pressure estimation. Before that I completed my PhD at the University of Notre Dame in the Computer Vision Research Lab. My research is at the intersection of signal processing, computer vision, and deep learning with applications in remote physiological measurement. I was fortunate to be advised by Adam Czajka and Patrick Flynn.

I’ve previously interned at Philips and Samsung Research America (SRA), in both cases working on camera-based sensing for cardiovascular monitoring.

News

Jul 8, 2024 I’m honored to have received the 2024 IEEE Biometrics Council Best Dissertation Award! It’s awarded once a year to only one PhD graduate (globally) working in biometrics. I will be presenting my PhD work at IJCB 2024 in Buffalo, NY between September 11th and 15th. Please reach out if you’re attending and want to talk anything related to camera-based physiology! I’m very grateful to my advisors Adam Czajka and Patrick Flynn and my collaborators in the CVRL at Notre Dame for their immense contributions to my dissertation throughout the PhD. Find my dissertation here if you are interested.
Nov 22, 2023 I’m excited to announce that I’ll be joining Presage Technologies as a Senior Research Scientist! It is bittersweet moving on from the CVRL, but I am eager to bring camera-based physiological measurement to the market for everyone to use. I’ll be primarily focusing on improving rPPG and blood pressure estimation from video.
Nov 15, 2023 After 4.5 years at University of Notre Dame, my time has come to an end. I succesfully defended my dissertation titled, “Remote Physiological Measurement in an Open World”. These last years have been the most fulfilling of my life. I am extremely grateful for my friends, colleagues, and advisors for all of their support along the way.
Aug 23, 2023 I gave an invited talk to Google X’s Tidal team on unsupervised learning from video (primarily focused on camera-based vitals). Many thanks to Sophia Abraham for inviting me, Cory Schillaci for organizing, and the rest of the Tidal team for great questions and discussions!
May 22, 2023 I have started a research intern position in the Digital Health Lab at Samsung Research America (SRA). My work will focus on algorithms to improve rPPG signal quality.
